Impacts of North Atlantic biases on the upper troposphere/lower stratosphere over the extratropical North Pacific

Many climate models struggle with persistent North Atlantic SST biases. This work asks whether those biases can project onto the North Pacific‚Äîespecially in the UTLS layer where jet-stream dynamics are sensitive.

Read more
Key results
  • Remote imprint: North Atlantic biases induce cooling below and warming in the UTLS over the extratropical North Pacific.
  • PDO-like structure: The resulting vertical dipole resembles the observed PDO-associated temperature structure.
  • Dynamical pathway: Tropical circulation changes help organize the extratropical North Pacific response.
