Research

Remote Climate Impacts of Atlantic Ocean Biases

The North Atlantic biases in coupled climate models significantly impact the atmospheric circulation in remote regions. In particular, this research explores how these biases influence the upper troposphere and lower stratosphere over the extratropical North Pacific. By altering storm track dynamics and eddy-driven jet structures, these changes can propagate through the entire climate system.

We demonstrate a clear pathway linking AMOC strength to Pacific atmospheric variability through both observational constraints and numerical simulations. Our published work (2023) highlights the upper-level wave response and implications for stratosphere–troposphere coupling.
